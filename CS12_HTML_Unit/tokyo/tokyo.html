Overview

Tokyo is the capital of japan and one of the most visited cities in the world. It blends ancient temples and shrines with toering skyscrapers and cutting-edge technology. Whether you love history, food, fashion, or nature, Tokyo has something for everyone.

Top Attractions

Tokyo has hundreds of things to see and do. Here are the must-see highlights:

  1. Senso-ji Temple - Tokyo's oldest and most famous buddhist temple in Asakusa
  2. Shibuya Crossing - The world's busiest pedestrian intersection
  3. Tokyo Skytree - a 634 -metre broadcasting tower with obseration decks
  4. Shinjuku Gyoen - A beautiful national garden perfect for cherry blossom season
  5. Harajuku - Famous for unique street fashion and Takeshita Stree shopping

For more information, visit the official tourism website: Go Tokyo

Food & Drink

Tokyo has more Michelin-starred restaurants than any other city in the world. Here are some foods you must try:

  • Ramen - Rich noodle soup available in dozens of regional styles
  • Sushi & Sashimi - Fresh fish served at evrything from conveyer belt restaurants to high-end counters
  • Takoyaki - Grilled octopus balls, a popular street food snack
  • Match everything - Green tea ice cream, cakes, lattes, and more
  • Yakitori - Grilled chicken skewers, best enjoyed at a local izakaya

Getting There

Tokyo is served by two major internation airports:

Airport Code Distance from City Centre Travel Time by Train
Narita International Airport NRT 60 km ~60 minutes
Haneda Airport HND 14 km ~25 minutes

Once in the city, the Tokyo Metro and JR train lines will take you almost anywhere. Consider purchasing a Suica card for easy tap-and-go travel on all trains and buses.
